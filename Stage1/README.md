# Part 2: Idea Development Process

## 🧠 Brainstorming Session

The idea development process for our team began with a structured brainstorming meeting that we scheduled in advance.

Each team member was asked to prepare several potential project ideas before the session, ensuring that the discussion would be productive and diverse.

During the meeting, every member presented their ideas one by one, allowing the team to explore a wide range of possibilities.

Although we did not formally apply specific brainstorming frameworks, the natural flow of our discussion reflected several well-known ideation techniques.

---

## 🗺️ Mind Mapping

As we compared and refined the proposed ideas, our conversation organically created a mind-mapping effect, where related concepts were grouped together, and connections between ideas became clearer.

This helped us identify common themes and understand how different suggestions aligned with one another.

---

## 🔄 SCAMPER Framework

Many of the questions raised during the discussion resembled elements of the SCAMPER framework.

Team members frequently asked whether certain ideas could be combined, modified, simplified, or adapted to better fit the project requirements.

These SCAMPER-like prompts allowed us to enhance and rethink several proposals, even if the method was not used explicitly.

---

## ❓ “How Might We” Questions

We also engaged in open-ended, exploratory questions like the “How Might We” approach.

Questions such as:

- “How might we choose an idea that is feasible within our timeline?”
- “How might we ensure the idea addresses a real user need?”

helped guide our thinking and encouraged creative problem-solving.

These questions supported the team in evaluating opportunities rather than focusing solely on limitations.

---

## ✅ Supervisor Feedback and Internal Voting

After completing the internal brainstorming session, we organized the ideas and presented them to our project supervisor for initial feedback.

The supervisor approved most of the proposed ideas, which allowed us to proceed to the next step: an internal voting process.

While the voting results and the final selected idea are documented in later sections, this brainstorming phase played a crucial role in shaping the direction of the project and ensuring that all team members contributed meaningfully to the ideation process.

## 💡 Idea 3: A Healthcare Directory Platform for Clinics and Hospitals in Saudi Arabia

### Description

A web platform that gathers clinics, hospitals, and healthcare centers in one place to help users easily search for medical services, compare options, and access reliable healthcare information.

The platform is especially useful for new visitors and residents in Saudi Arabia who may struggle to find trusted doctors or suitable healthcare providers.

### Strengths

- Solves a real problem for both residents and international visitors.
- There is currently no widely known platform in Saudi Arabia that provides the same centralized healthcare experience.
- Helps users discover trusted doctors, clinics, and hospitals more easily.
- Supports healthcare awareness and improves accessibility to medical services.
- Strong future potential for expansion:
  - Doctor ratings
  - Appointment booking
  - Multilingual support

### Weaknesses

- Requires accurate and updated healthcare data from hospitals and clinics.
- Difficulty obtaining partnerships or official databases from healthcare providers.
- Managing and verifying medical information can be time-consuming.
- Limited technical integration without access to official healthcare APIs.
- The platform may initially remain informational only without direct booking features.

### The Reasons for Rejection

- The project would require real hospital databases and official healthcare partnerships, which are difficult to obtain within the project timeline.
- Data collection and verification would add significant complexity to the development process.
- The team decided to prioritize the main project idea because it better matched the project requirements and gained majority agreement among team members.
- The selected project idea was considered more feasible within the available time and technical resources.

# Part 4: Selected MVP Concept

## 🥗 Describe the MVP Idea

The MVP is a simple web platform that brings together healthy restaurants offering weekly or monthly meal subscription plans, such as work lunch plans or full-day healthy meal plans.

Instead of subscribing to only one restaurant for the whole month, users can subscribe through one platform and choose meals from different healthy restaurants on different days.

For example, a user may choose a meal from **Calo** on Sunday, **Greenola** on Monday, and **Fresh House** on Tuesday, all under one subscription plan.

The MVP will focus on the simplest version of the idea. It will include restaurant listings, meal selection, subscription request creation, and user account management.

It will not include online payment, real delivery tracking, or direct restaurant system integration in the first version.

---

## ❗ The Problem It Solves

Many people who want to eat healthy meals, especially employees, students, and busy individuals, face a common problem: most healthy meal subscriptions require them to commit to one restaurant for a full week or month.

This can become repetitive and boring, which may cause users to stop following their meal plan.

Another problem is convenience. A busy employee may not have time to order food every day from different restaurants, enter the delivery address repeatedly, or communicate with different delivery drivers.

Companies may also find it difficult to arrange employee discounts with multiple healthy restaurants separately.

This MVP solves the problem by offering one platform where users can create a flexible subscription and select meals from different healthy restaurants in one place.

---

## 🎯 Target Audience or Users

Employees, Students, and any person want to maintain a healthier lifestyle through planned meals.

## 📌 MVP Summary, Rationale, and Potential Impact

This MVP is a web-based platform that allows users to subscribe to healthy meals from multiple restaurants through one flexible subscription plan.

The rationale behind the idea is that current healthy meal subscription services usually require users to commit to one restaurant for a long period, which can lead to boring and lower commitment.

From a technical perspective, the project was selected because it allows us to apply important full-stack development skills such as front-end design, back-end APIs, database design, authentication, and CRUD operations.

From a business perspective, the idea introduces a new layer of convenience by combining subscription management, meal variety, and healthy food options in one platform.

The potential impact of this MVP is significant. It can improve the experience of users who want to eat healthy but need more variety and convenience.

It can also help employees save time during workdays and support healthier eating habits.

In the future, the platform could grow into a marketplace connecting users, healthy restaurants, and companies that want to offer food-related benefits to their employees.

---

## ✅ Reasons for Selection

### 1. Feasibility

The idea is feasible for a student project because the first version can be implemented with limited scope.

The MVP does not require real payment processing, real delivery integration, or actual restaurant system connections.

The team can use sample restaurant and meal data stored in a database.

The project matches common full-stack development requirements:

| Technical Area | Application in the Project |
|---|---|
| Front-End | Pages for restaurants, meals, subscription plan, and order summary. |
| Back-End | APIs for users, restaurants, meals, and subscription requests. |
| Database | Tables for users, restaurants, meals, addresses, and subscriptions. |
| Authentication | User sign-up and login. |
| CRUD Operations | Create, read, update, and delete records. |

---

### 2. Innovation

The innovative part of the idea is not simply offering healthy food online.

The innovation is the multi-restaurant subscription model.

Instead of forcing users to subscribe to one restaurant for the whole month, the platform gives them flexibility and variety within one subscription.

This makes the project different from traditional food delivery apps because it focuses on planned subscriptions, convenience, and variety rather than one-time food orders.

---

### 3. Alignment with Holberton Project Goals

The idea aligns well with Holberton’s final project goals because it allows the team to demonstrate multiple software engineering skills, including:

1. Solving a real-world problem.
2. Designing a clear user flow.
3. Building a full-stack web application.
4. Creating and managing a database.
5. Developing RESTful APIs.
6. Implementing authentication.
7. Presenting a scalable product idea.
